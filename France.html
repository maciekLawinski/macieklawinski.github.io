PLEY,PARIS (Romantic)

Located in the heart of Paris, on the famous Rue du Faubourg Saint Honoré, PLEY Hotel is an eco-friendly hotel which offers luxurious guest rooms and a lounge area. Each en suite room has free Wi-Fi access and a complementary bottle of mineral water. The guest rooms are decorated in a contemporary style. Some of the rooms offer views over the Parisian rooftops. A breakfast buffet is served every morning, which can be taken in the lounge area or in the comfort of your room. PLEY Hotel is close to the Champs-Elysées, Arc de Triomphe and the Louvre Museum. Ternes Metro Station is 550 metres from the property, while Charles de Gaulle Etoile Metro Station is 650 metres away..

NOVOTEL PARIS LES HALLES (Family)

The contemporary 4-star Novotel Paris Les Halles sits in the historic heart of the capital, opposite Saint Eustache church and Les Halles gardens. With excellent public transport links and iconic attractions like Notre-Dame, La Samaritaine and the Louvre within walking distance, this modern hotel is an ideal choice for couples seeking a romantic city break or families looking for a comfortable base to explore Paris.

Le Gabriel (romantic )

Within the gold and bronze setting of Le Gabriel, Jérôme Banctel has crafted a deeply personal cuisine – calmly creative, subtly technical, and always focused on the product, which is sourced from his native Brittany or French forests during game season. Here, there are no grandiose menu titles or overuse of luxury ingredients as a cover-up. Instead, he moves diners with a ‘simple’ dish of Bretagne carrot des sables, an artichoke (glazed with cherry vinegar), or squid (slow-cooked in duck fat). You won’t find a more evocative dinner for such a good price anywhere else in Paris. .

Chez Janou (Adventure)

If you’re craving that quintessential Parisian bistro vibe with a side of southern French flair, Chez Janou is your spot. Tucked away in the Marais, this place is always buzzing – think vintage décor, checkered floor tiles, and tables squeezed shoulder-to-shoulder. Their chocolate mousse is legendary (served from a giant bowl at your table), but don’t sleep on the classic ratatouille or the generously-loaded Niçoise salad. The pastis selection is wild; you’ll find bottles here you’ve never even heard of. Best tables are on the leafy terrace for people-watching.
