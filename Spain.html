Spain

Hotels

The Hat Madrid (Student)

This hotel is honestly perfect for students. It’s super affordable but still really comfortable, and even though it’s a 2-star place, it has everything you actually need. They offer shared rooms, which means you get a great social vibe and plenty of chances to meet people and make awesome memories. You get 24/7 reception, free super-fast Wi-Fi, laundry facilities, secure lockers, and loads of other useful stuff. The Hat Madrid isn’t close to a beach — you’ll need to hop on a bus or train — but it makes up for that with the nightlife. The bar district is only about a 10-minute walk away, so there’s always something fun to do.

Bahia del Duque (Family)

The 5-star Bahía del Duque Resort is located in the stunning Costa Adeje area of Tenerife. Just a short walk from a beautiful beach with crystal-clear waters and soft, clean sand, this resort is perfect for a luxurious family getaway. The hotel offers a wide range of facilities, including local shops stocked with essentials like sunscreen and toys, and stylish air-conditioned rooms with beautiful, comfortable furnishings. Families will love that the resort is child-friendly, with pools for both kids and adults, fun competitions, and games organized by the staff. For parents seeking a little relaxation, a babysitting service is also available, allowing you to enjoy some quiet time knowing your little ones are in safe hands.

Parador Corias (Romantic)

The 4-star Parador de Corias is in Cangas del Narcea and mixes modern vibes with amazing natural scenery. They’ve got free Wi-Fi, big comfy rooms with flat-screen TVs, and awesome views of the surroundings — perfect if you want to relax but still have some privacy. Bathrooms are spacious with a bathtub and bidet and include complimentary toiletries. The restaurant is elegant and quite romantic, so it’s a great spot for couples to have a nice dinner. There’s also a spa with a Turkish bath and sauna, plus a heated indoor pool perfect for chilling out or doing some low-key exercise together. Overall, it’s a calm and romantic place for couples to unwind and enjoy themselves.

Parador de Fuente Dé (Adventure)

The 3-star Parador de Fuente Dé is right in the middle of the stunning Camaleño National Park, making it the perfect spot for a peaceful mountain getaway. The hotel has a lot of traditional charm, and most of it is made of wood, giving it that cozy, nature-vibe feel. Surrounded by mountains and forests, it’s a paradise for adventure lovers — think epic hikes, breathtaking views of the valley, and sunsets that will literally melt your heart. If you’re someone who loves being outdoors, this place is a total win.

Restaurants

El Tigre (Student)

A great budget-friendly spot, this tapas bar offers a generous plate of tapas with every drink you order. The portions are surprisingly large for the price, making it an excellent choice for anyone looking to enjoy good food without spending much. Its lively atmosphere and unbeatable value have made it especially popular with students, and you’ll often find the bar buzzing with groups sharing plates and drinks late into the evening.

Disfrutar (Romantic)

Disfrutar is such a cool spot for a romantic night. The food is amazing and really creative, and everything looks super pretty on the plate. The vibe is chill but fancy, so it feels special without being too serious. Definitely a place where you and your date can just enjoy the food and have a great time together.

El Jardín de la Máquina (Family)

El Jardín de la Máquina is a really nice spot for a family meal. The food is delicious and there’s something for everyone, so even kids will find something they like. The atmosphere is relaxed and friendly, which makes it easy to enjoy time together and have a fun, stress-free meal with family.

Gastroescaperoom (Adventure)

Gastroescaperoom is a really fun place for an adventure with friends. You get to solve puzzles and challenges while enjoying amazing food, so it’s like an escape room and a meal rolled into one. The experience is exciting and different from anywhere else, making it a perfect way to spend a few hours laughing, thinking, and trying new dishes together.

Landmarks

Playa d’en Bossa (Student)

Playa d’en Bossa is one of the most popular holiday spots on the south-east coast of Ibiza. It has a long, 2.7 km beach, lots of bars and clubs, and it’s very close to Ibiza Town and the airport. It’s a lively place with plenty to do from watersports and beach clubs during the day to famous superclubs at night. Even though it’s known for partying, there are also quieter parts of the beach.

Park Güell (Romantic)

Park Güell isn’t just a normal public park — it feels like stepping into a fairy-tale. Known for its bright, colorful mosaics, winding shapes, and unique buildings, it’s one of Gaudí’s most famous works. As you walk around, you’ll find amazing views over Barcelona and lots of fun, creative details everywhere you look. It’s both a beautiful green space and a true piece of art.

City of Arts and Sciences (Family)

The City of Arts and Sciences is a large modern cultural and architectural complex in Valencia. It was built on the old riverbed of the Turia and includes a science museum, a planetarium, an opera house, and L’Oceanogràfic — the biggest aquarium in Europe! The whole area is famous for its futuristic white buildings and is one of the city’s most popular places to visit.

Caminito del Rey (Adventure)

This almost 8-kilometre path was once known as one of the most dangerous in the world. Today it has been completely restored and is much safer, but one thing is still the same — the amazing feeling of walking along pathways hanging over 100 metres high on the side of a cliff. The views are truly unforgettable.
